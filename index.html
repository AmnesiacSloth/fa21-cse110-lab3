Agenda

  1. Overview of positions

    Setters - Run the offense for our team, Outside Hitters - Main outlets for attacking, should also be stellar defensively , Middle Blockers - First line of defense against opposing teams' threats, must be fast at attacking on transition plays, Libero - Defensive specialist, must be quick and able to react to opposing teams attacks on the fly, Opposite Hitter - Strong offensive option, must also be good blocker as they will be matched up against opposing team's outside hitters

  2. Rotation 1

    Rotation 1 allows for lots of flexibility in player positioning. In traditional 5-1 offensive systems the setter has a full front row of attacking options. Another benefit of Rotation 1 is that against especially tough serving teams, we can pull a player from the net and have a 4 man backrow for serve-recieve. Setters can also have their wing attackers move along the net to free them up and cause disruption for the enemy's blocking schema. Additionally, having the libero in zone 2 for serve-recieve means that servers will be hard pressed to avoid feeding our team's best passer
